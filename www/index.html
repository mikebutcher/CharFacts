Scalefree or AccuBlue 35

Back

Scalefree or AccuBlue 35

  • 20 ml/100 gallons of makeup divided by cycles
  • Scale control
  • Good for high skin temps/process systems
  • Only choice when skin temps exceed 160 F
  • Bulk water temps >100 F
  • High calcium and high stress
  • Suitable for use with halogens
  • Control range Mo 1-1.5 ppm
  • Control range blue 1.5-2.0 ppm
  • PBTC as active phosphonate

Scalefree or AccuBlue 38

  • 25 ml/100 gallons of makeup divided by cycles
  • Scale and Corrosion inhibition
  • Comfort cooling
  • <500 ppm calcium
  • Skin temps <160 F
  • Water temps <100 F
  • Suitable for use with halogens
  • Control range Mo 1-1.5 ppm
  • Control range blue 1.5-2.0 ppm
  • PBTC/POCA as active phosphonate

Stabrom 909

Back
  • 4 oz per 1000 gallons of system water
  • 6.4 ppm as bromine per dose
  • Recommended lockout is 90 minutes
  • Stabilized bromine
  • DO NOT OVERFEED!!!
  • Feed schedule only
  • Do not use as shock treatment
  • Check chemical injectors monthly
  • Point chemical injectors down
  • Recommend degassing head pump to avoid prime loss

Back

JustEQ 07

Back
  • 2 oz per 1000 gallons of system water
  • Recommended lockout is 90 minutes
  • Stabilized chlorine/bromine
  • DO NOT OVERFEED!!!
  • Feed schedule only
  • Do not use as shock treatment
  • Check chemical injectors monthly
  • Point chemical injectors down
  • Use degassing head pump to avoid prime loss

Bromine Tabs

Back
  • 1-3 ppm as bromine tested
  • Recommended lockout is 90 minutes
  • No lockout required when fed on ORP
  • Solid Bromine
  • Feed by Brominator/tower duck
  • Feed schedule/% time/ORP
  • Big Bro pressure rating 80 psi
  • Neptune pressure rating 150 psi

XtraBrom 111

Back
  • 1-3 ppm as bromine tested
  • Recommended lockout is 90 minutes
  • No lockout required when fed on ORP
  • Higher bromine yield per pound
  • Solid Bromine (granular or tabs)
  • Feed by Brominator/tower duck
  • Feed schedule/% time/ORP
  • Big Bro pressure rating 80 psi
  • Neptune pressure rating 150 psi

InduChlor

Back
  • Extremely soluble; use only as shock treatment
  • Calcium hypochlorite legal for use in towers
  • Start at 1 pound per 1,000 gallons

Chlor-L

Back
  • Antifoulant; not a biocide
  • Liquid solution of 17% hypochlorite
  • Use as shock treatment or feed to 1-2 ppm as chlorine
  • Tends to outgas and expand container
  • Short shelf life made worse by heat; use before 6 months
  • Feed schedule/% time/ORP
  • Use degassing head pump to avoid prime loss

Tolcide PS-20A

Back
  • Combination of biocide and biopenetrant
  • Use 5 to 20 oz per 1000 gallons for maintenance dose
  • Recommended lockout is 2 hours
  • Use up to 1 gallon per 1000 gallons for fouled systems
  • Use on feed schedule only
  • Especially effective against SRB
  • Cost effective choice on fluid coolers low light towers

Busan 77 (60% WSCP, Slimex B IV)

Back
  • Use 2 to 3 oz per 1000 gallons for maintenance dose
  • Recommended lockout is 2 hours
  • Use on feed schedule only
  • Not so good against fouling or dirty water
  • Long half life makes it a good choice for closed loops
  • Avoid using with sand filters
  • Cost effective choice on fluid coolers low light towers


Busan 881 (50% NABEM, Slimex E II)

Back
  • Use 2 to 3 oz per 1000 gallons for maintenance dose
  • Recommended lockout is 2 hours
  • Use on feed schedule only
  • Avoid use in systems with high iron or other metal levels
  • Hazardous reaction when exposed to other chemicals
  • Cost effective choice on fluid coolers low light towers

Filmex 100

Back
  • Biopenetrant for loosening and removing biofilm
  • Use 6 to 25 oz per 1000 gallons for maintenance dose
  • Recommended lockout is 2 hours
  • Use on feed schedule only
  • Do not use alone
  • Commonly used along with a real biocide

Busan Something (MECT-5, Algex 200)

Back
  • Use 5 to 15 oz per 1000 gallons for maintenance dose
  • Recommended lockout is 2 hours
  • Use on feed schedule only
  • Especially effective against algae and in wooden towers
  • Avoid PVC and acrylic pump and injector components
  • Never inject upstream of controller

Sterilex

Back
  • Prescription product; consult Technical Support Staff first
  • Recommended for fouled loops and tower cleanings
  • Supplied as two separate solutions
  • Solution 1 is the active product; Solution 2 is just a pH buffer
  • Use Solution 2 only when pH is below 8.3
  • When using Solution 2, use it first and allow to circulate fully
  • Use 0.5 to 1.0 gallon per 1000 gallons for most cleaning projects
  • Recommended lockout is 2 hours

Sump Buddies

Back
  • Prescription product; consult Technical Support Staff first
  • Feed 1 puck per 1000 gallons using Tower Duck or onion bag
  • Make sure feeder does not touch metal surfaces
  • Recommended for fouled loops and tower cleanings
  • Supplied as pucks in 25 pound pails
  • Quick kill biocide with a very short half life
  • Bleed lockout is not needed

BD-1

Back
  • 1 lb sulfite per gallon
  • Liquid Sulfite
  • Use rate varies by load and application
  • Sulfite is cheap, pitted tubes are expensive
  • Feed as far upstream as possible
  • One year shelf life
  • Will freeze in the winter if left on truck
  • Can be blended with all boiler chemicals

BD-6

Back
  • Liquid sodium hydroxide
  • Delivers 5.6 lbs of NaOH per gallon
  • Used to control pH and hydroxide alkalinity
  • Feed into feed tank/deareator
  • Can be used to neutralize acidic solutions
  • Use proper PPE when handling (long gloves)

BJ-10

Back
  • Solid sodium sulfite (catalyzed)
  • Can be mixed up to 1.5 lbs per gallon in batch tank
  • Use in a stand alone batch tank when possible
  • Agitator needed to properly mix
  • Use care when running agitator not to lose sulfite

BH-10

Back
  • Solid sodium hydroxide
  • Agitator needed to properly mix
  • Keep in dry location and is very hydroscopic
  • Used to control pH and hydroxide alkalinity
  • Feed into feed tank/deareator
  • Can be used to neutralize acidic solutions
  • Use proper PPE when handling (long gloves)

BH-10

Back

SN-7

Back

  • Use 2.5 gallons per 1000 gallons of system water
  • Oxygen scavenger and metal passivator
  • Used on chilled closed loop systems
  • Control range is 800-1200 ppm as Nitrite

SN-10

Back
  • Corrosion inhibitor
  • Nitrite, azole, polymer, limited caustic
  • Use 5 gallons per 1000 gallons of system water
  • Oxygen scavenger and metal passivator
  • Used on hot closed loop systems
  • Control range is 1500-2000 ppm as Nitrite
  • If combination hot/chilled, use SN-10

SN-150

Back
  • Corrosion inhibitor
  • Hydrazine, azole, polymer, limited caustic
  • Use 1 quart per 1000 gallons of system water
  • Can be used in low conductivity loops
  • Use in systems with high mild steel corrosion
  • Convert hematite to magnetite
  • Superior oxygen scavenger and metal passivator
  • Used on chilled/hot closed loop systems
  • Control range is 5-10 ppm as hydrazine
  • Requires a filter
  • Listed as suspected carcinogen
  • Cheap and low use rate

CharPrep Plus

Charclean

  • Removes oily deposits

Charsperse 100

DA-11

Alkalinity

Back
  • 25 ml sample
  • Add Alkalinity Indicator (R-101) to appropriate color (approx. 5 drops)
  • Count drops of Sulfuric Acid (R-105) until color changes from blue to pink
  • Multiply number of drops of R-105 times 20

Ammonia

Back
  • Filter sample if necessary
  • 5 ml in one #0230 test tube
  • Add 5 drops of Rochelle Salt Solution (R-182)
  • Add 5 drops of Nessler Reagent (R-183)
  • Invert to mix
  • WAIT 2 MINUTES
  • Use Hydrazine Comparator
  • Multiply value on comparator by 10

Bromine

Back
  • Fill sample cup to 25 ml mark
  • Add 5 drops of A-1600 Activator solution
  • WAIT ONE MINUTE
  • Snap one Chemet ampoule against side of sample cup
  • Ampoule will fill with sample
  • Wait 2 minutes to develop color
  • Match color in comparator

Calcium Hardness(Low Range)

Back
  • 25 ml sample
  • Add Calcium Hardness Buffer (R-109) until sample PH = 12
  • Add Calcium Hardness Indicator (R-112) to appropriate color
  • Count drops of EDTA (R-111) magenta color turns permanently to royal blue
  • Multiply number of drops of EDTA Solution (R-111) times 20

Calcium Hardness (High Range)

Back
  • 1 ml sample diluted with 24 ml of Deionized water
  • Add Calcium Hardness Buffer (R-109) until sample PH = 12
  • Add Calcium Hardness Indicator (R-112) to appropriate color
  • Count drops of EDTA (R-111P) until color turns permanently to royal blue
  • Multiply number of drops of EDTA Solution (R-111P) times 25

Chlorides

Back
  • 15, 30 or 60 ml sample
  • Add 1 drop of Phenolphthalein (R-102), if clear skip next step
  • Add Sulfuric Acid (R-105) until sample clears
  • Add 12 drops of Chloride Indicator (R-151)
  • Count drops of Chloride Titrant (R-153) to a permanent rusty color
  • 1 drop = 2.5 ppm in 60 ml sample
  • 1 drop = 5 ppm in 30 ml sample
  • 1 drop = 10 ppm in 15 ml sample

Conductivity Boiler

Back
  • Pour 25 ml sample into beaker
  • Cool sample to < 100 F
  • Add 2 drops of Phenolphthalein (R-102) and swirl to mix
  • Add drops of Neutralizing Solution (R-128) until sample turns clear
  • Test in conductivity meter

Copper

Back
  • Filter sample if necessary
  • 5 ml in two #1730 test tube
  • Add 1 Copper Reagent pillow to right tube
  • Compare in color wheel

Hydrazine

Back
  • Dilute sample 1:24 in Grad. Cylinder
  • Add 5 ml of diluted solution to #0230 test tube
  • Add 25 drops of Hydrazine Reagent 1 (R-146)
  • Add 3 scoops of Hydrazine Reagent 2 to tall #0822 test tube
  • Pour #0230 test tube into #0822 test tube
  • Shake until dissolved
  • WAIT 10 MINUTES
  • Match color in comparator
  • Multiply matching value by 25

Iron

Back
  • Filter sample if necessary
  • 5 ml in two #1730 test tubes
  • Add 1 Iron Reagent pillow to right tube
  • Compare in color wheel

Molybdate

Back
  • Thoroughly rinse test tubes
  • 5 ml in two #1730 test tubes
  • 1 Molybdate pillow in right tube
  • Allow to dissolve completely
  • Add 14 drops Molybdenum 2 Reagent (R-120) to right tube
  • WAIT 2 MINUTES
  • Compare in color wheel

Nitrite

Back
  • Pour 5 ml into #1730 test tube
  • Add 8 drops of Ferroin Indicator (R-160) to turn orange
  • Count drops of CAN Reagent (R-162) until orange turns light blue

pH Cooling Tower

Back
  • Use Thymol Blue comparator
  • 5 ml in #0230 test tube
  • Add 2 drops of Thymol Blue Solution (R-107)
  • Match color in comparator (If PH < 8, use Cresol Red)

pH Condensate

Back
  • Use Cresol Red comparator
  • 5 ml in #0230 test tube
  • Add 2 drops of Cresol Red Solution (R-107)
  • Match color in comparator(If PH > 8.4, use Thymol Blue)

Steam Boiler Alkalinity

Back
  • 25 ml sample
  • Add Phenolphthalein (R-102) until sample turns Pink
  • Count drops of R-105 until color clears (This is the P-Alkalinity)
  • Add R-101 to appropriate color
  • Count drops of R-105 until color changes from blue to pink
  • Multiple the total number of drops of R-105 from all steps times 20
  • OH-ALK = 2 times (P-ALK minus M-ALK)

Sulfite

Back
  • Cool 25 ml sample to < 100 F
  • Completely dissolve 1 Sulfite Reagent 1 Pillow
  • Dissolve 1 scoop of sulfamic acid (R-126)
  • Count drops of Sulfite Reagent 3 (R-127) until permanent dark blue color
  • Multiply number of drops by 4

Total Hardness

Back
  • 25 ml sample
  • Add Total Hardness Buffer (R-110) until sample PH = 12
  • Add Total Hardness Indicator (R-113) to medium blue color
  • Count drops of EDTA (R-111) until color turns permanently royal blue
  • Multiple number of drops of EDTA (R-111) times 20

Calibrating LMI Pump with System Running

Back

Calibrating LMI Pump with System Running

  • Connect discharge line to injector
  • Connect 1/4” suction tubing to 30 mL Syringe
  • Fill syringe to 30 mL mark with water
  • Count the number of seconds it takes to pump 30 mL
  • Divide 1800 by this number to determine output
  • Result is output in milliliters per minute

Example: 36 seconds. 1800/ 36 = 50 mL/min.
Remember: To convert milliliters to ounces, multiply mL by 0.0338
